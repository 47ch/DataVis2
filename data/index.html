Internet Usage Across the World

As technology progresses, people all around the world become further exposed to the benefits of being constantly connected to the Internet. Some countries went through a rapid transformation, as the internet gradually seeps into all aspects of their lives. Some countries did not have sufficient resources to adapt it into their society, and so took a slow and steady approach to it instead. This visualisation serves to showcase how quickly people across the world gained access to the internet.

Individual Countries' Adoption of Broadband

In some countries, being able to access the internet is the least of their worries. Certain countries may be war torn, resulting in a lack of infrastructure for broadband to even function properly. Others may have a majority of their citizens poor, to the point where they struggle to even put food on the table. The luxury of owning a device with internet connectability becomes a foolish thought in front of these people. As such, countries where most of the citizens have access to food, water and shelter tend to have a higher percentage of their population owning a broadband subscription.
